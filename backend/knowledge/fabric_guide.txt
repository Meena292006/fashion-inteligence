COMPLETE FABRIC GUIDE - PROPERTIES, CARE, AND STYLING

=== NATURAL FIBERS ===

COTTON - The Universal Fabric
Properties:
- Breathable and absorbent (absorbs up to 27 times its weight in water)
- Soft and comfortable against skin
- Durable and strong, gets stronger when wet
- Hypoallergenic, perfect for sensitive skin
- Takes dye very well, holds color
- Wrinkles easily unless treated
- Can shrink 3-5% if not pre-shrunk

Types of Cotton:
Egyptian Cotton - extra-long staple, very soft, luxury bedding and shirts
Pima Cotton - similar to Egyptian, grown in Americas, silky and durable
Organic Cotton - no pesticides, environmentally friendly, slightly more expensive
Supima Cotton - American Pima, very soft and color-retentive

Best For:
- Everyday t-shirts, casual shirts, underwear
- Summer clothing (breathable)
- Workout clothes when blended with synthetics
- Jeans and casual pants
- Bedding and towels

Care Instructions:
- Machine wash warm or cold
- Tumble dry low or hang dry to prevent shrinkage
- Iron while slightly damp for easier wrinkle removal
- Separate whites from colors first few washes
- Add vinegar to wash to set colors

Styling Tips:
- Perfect for casual, approachable looks
- Crisp white cotton shirt is a wardrobe essential
- Cotton poplin for structured shirts
- Cotton jersey for comfortable t-shirts
- Pair with denim, chinos, or casual skirts

---

LINEN - The Summer Essential
Properties:
- Most breathable natural fabric, 3x more breathable than cotton
- Extremely absorbent, wicks moisture away
- Dries very quickly
- Gets softer and more comfortable with each wash
- Naturally antibacterial and hypoallergenic
- Wrinkles VERY easily (this is part of the charm)
- Can be stiff when new
- 2-3 times stronger than cotton

Types of Linen:
Irish Linen - highest quality, fine and tightly woven
Belgian Linen - also high quality, smooth texture
French Linen - softer, slightly looser weave
European Linen - general term for high-quality linen

Best For:
- Summer suits, blazers, pants
- Beach and resort wear
- Casual button-up shirts
- Dresses and skirts
- Bedding (stays cool in summer)
- Tablecloths and napkins

Care Instructions:
- Hand wash or machine wash cold/warm on gentle cycle
- Do NOT wring or twist, gently squeeze excess water
- Hang dry or lay flat (tumble drying causes severe wrinkling)
- Iron while still damp with high heat and steam
- Never bleach (weakens fibers)
- Dry clean for structured linen items

Styling Tips:
- Embrace the wrinkles - crisp linen looks try-hard
- Perfect for the "relaxed sophistication" aesthetic
- Pair linen shirts with cotton or denim bottoms
- Neutral linen (white, beige, navy) is most versatile
- Avoid linen for formal events (unless specifically requested)
- Layer with cotton pieces for textural interest

---

WOOL - The Winter Workhorse
Properties:
- Naturally insulating, traps warm air
- Moisture-wicking, pulls sweat away from body
- Water-resistant when tightly woven
- Naturally elastic, resists wrinkles
- Flame-resistant, won't melt
- Can be itchy on sensitive skin
- Shrinks and felts if washed improperly
- Biodegradable and sustainable

Types of Wool:
Merino Wool - soft, fine, non-itchy, perfect for base layers
Cashmere - from cashmere goats, extremely soft, luxury fiber, expensive
Lambswool - from sheep's first shearing, soft and elastic
Shetland Wool - coarser, very warm, traditional sweater wool
Alpaca Wool - softer than sheep's wool, hypoallergenic, very warm
Mohair - from angora goats, silky and lustrous, blends well

Best For:
- Suits and blazers
- Winter coats and peacoats
- Sweaters and cardigans
- Dress pants
- Scarves and beanies
- Base layers for extreme cold (merino)
- Formal wear (wool gabardine)

Care Instructions:
- Dry clean for structured items (suits, coats)
- Hand wash delicate knits in cold water with wool-specific detergent
- Never wring, gently press water out
- Lay flat to dry (hanging stretches the fabric)
- Use moth balls or cedar for storage
- Air out instead of washing when possible
- Steam to refresh and remove odors

Styling Tips:
- Wool suits are the foundation of professional wardrobes
- Merino sweaters can be worn next to skin comfortably
- Mix wool textures (smooth gabardine pants with chunky knit sweater)
- Charcoal and navy wool are most versatile
- Wool coats are investment pieces, buy quality
- Pair with cotton shirts for classic layering

---

SILK - The Luxury Fiber
Properties:
- Smooth, soft, and lustrous
- Naturally temperature-regulating (cool in summer, warm in winter)
- Strongest natural fiber, very durable
- Absorbent but dries quickly
- Takes dye beautifully, rich colors
- Delicate, can water-spot and snag
- Expensive
- Degrades in sunlight over time

Types of Silk:
Mulberry Silk - highest quality, uniform texture
Charmeuse - satin-like finish, drapey and elegant
Chiffon - sheer and lightweight, for blouses and scarves
Crepe de Chine - matte finish, slightly textured
Raw Silk - nubby texture, more casual
Dupioni - crisp with slubs, for formal wear

Best For:
- Blouses and dressy shirts
- Ties and pocket squares
- Evening gowns and cocktail dresses
- Lingerie and sleepwear
- Scarves
- Lining for jackets and coats
- Pajamas (luxury)

Care Instructions:
- Dry clean for best results
- Hand wash with gentle silk detergent if you must
- Never wring, roll in towel to remove excess water
- Air dry away from direct sunlight
- Iron on lowest setting while damp, inside out
- Store away from light to prevent yellowing
- Don't spray perfume directly on silk (causes stains)

Styling Tips:
- Silk blouses elevate any outfit instantly
- Pair silk tops with structured bottoms (denim, wool pants)
- Silk scarves add elegance without bulk
- Avoid silk in rain (water spots)
- Great for special occasions and evening wear
- Mix silk with casual fabrics for high-low styling

=== SYNTHETIC FIBERS ===

POLYESTER - The Practical Choice
Properties:
- Very durable and resistant to shrinking and stretching
- Wrinkle-resistant
- Quick-drying
- Holds pleats and creases well
- Doesn't breathe well, can trap heat and odor
- Can feel clammy in heat
- Inexpensive
- Resists mildew and moths

Best For:
- Workout clothes (moisture-wicking when treated)
- Outerwear (water-resistant)
- Wrinkle-free travel clothes
- Blended with cotton for easy-care shirts
- Linings
- Affordable formal wear

Care Instructions:
- Machine wash warm
- Tumble dry low
- Remove from dryer promptly to avoid wrinkles
- Iron on low if needed
- Avoid high heat (melts)

Styling Tips:
- Look for poly blends, not 100% polyester (feels cheaper)
- Great for pieces you need to wash frequently
- Modern performance polyester doesn't look as cheap as it used to
- Avoid for summer heat (doesn't breathe)

---

NYLON - The Sport Fabric
Properties:
- Extremely strong and durable
- Elastic and flexible
- Quick-drying
- Wrinkle and abrasion-resistant
- Lightweight
- Can feel synthetic and clammy
- Doesn't breathe well

Best For:
- Activewear and swimwear
- Windbreakers and rain jackets
- Tights and hosiery
- Backpacks and luggage
- Blended with spandex for stretch

Care Instructions:
- Machine wash cold
- Hang dry or tumble dry low
- Avoid high heat
- Hand wash delicates

Styling Tips:
- Purely functional, not typically stylish alone
- Nylon jackets for outdoor activities
- Works in athleisure looks

---

SPANDEX/LYCRA/ELASTANE - The Stretch Fiber
Properties:
- Can stretch 5-8 times its original size
- Always blended with other fibers (never 100%)
- Retains shape after stretching
- Lightweight and smooth
- Doesn't breathe on its own

Best For:
- Activewear (leggings, sports bras)
- Stretch jeans (1-3% spandex)
- Fitted dresses and tops
- Shapewear
- Swimwear

Care Instructions:
- Follow instructions for the primary fiber
- Avoid chlorine bleach (breaks down elastic)
- Avoid high heat (damages elasticity)

Styling Tips:
- Small amounts of spandex add comfort without looking "stretchy"
- Jeans with 2% spandex fit better and feel more comfortable
- Avoid high-spandex items for formal settings

---

=== BLENDED FABRICS ===

COTTON-POLYESTER BLENDS (60/40 or 50/50)
Benefits:
- Combines breathability of cotton with durability of polyester
- Less wrinkling than pure cotton
- More comfortable than pure polyester
- More affordable
- Easy care

Best For:
- Dress shirts for work
- Travel clothing
- Children's clothes (durable)

---

WOOL-SYNTHETIC BLENDS
Benefits:
- Maintains wool's warmth and appearance
- More affordable than 100% wool
- Easier care, less shrinkage
- Can be machine washed (depending on blend)

Best For:
- Business suits (wool-poly)
- Casual blazers
- Winter coats

---

=== SPECIALTY FABRICS ===

DENIM - The American Classic
Properties:
- Made from cotton twill
- Durable and sturdy
- Gets softer with wear
- Fades over time (desirable for some)
- Can be stiff when new

Types:
Raw/Selvedge Denim - unwashed, no pre-fading, higher quality
Stretch Denim - includes 1-3% spandex for comfort
Distressed Denim - pre-worn look, rips and fading

Care:
- Wash inside out in cold water
- Air dry to prevent shrinkage
- Raw denim enthusiasts wash rarely (every 6 months)
- Dark denim can bleed, wash separately

Styling:
- Dark wash = more formal, light wash = casual
- Fit matters more than brand
- Cuffing adds personality
- Pair with almost anything

---

LEATHER - The Luxury Material
Properties:
- Durable and long-lasting
- Develops patina with age
- Water-resistant (when treated)
- Breathable
- Expensive
- Requires special care

Types:
Full-Grain Leather - highest quality, shows natural texture
Top-Grain Leather - sanded smooth, slightly less durable
Genuine Leather - lower quality, cheaper
Suede - soft, napped finish, less water-resistant

Care:
- Condition regularly with leather conditioner
- Avoid water, dry immediately if wet
- Store with shape support (shoe trees, hangers)
- Professional cleaning for major issues

Styling:
- Leather jackets are timeless
- Leather shoes should be rotated (let rest between wears)
- Mix leather with soft fabrics for contrast

---

VELVET - The Opulent Fabric
Properties:
- Soft pile surface
- Luxurious drape
- Can crush and show wear patterns
- Usually dry clean only

Best For:
- Evening wear
- Formal blazers
- Winter accessories
- Statement furniture

Styling:
- Rich jewel tones for maximum impact
- Pair with matte fabrics to let velvet shine
- Great for holiday and winter styling

=== FABRIC CARE SUMMARY ===

Always Check Labels:
- Symbols tell you: wash temp, bleach ok?, tumble dry?, iron temp, dry clean only

General Rules:
- Cold water preserves colors and prevents shrinkage
- Hot water for whites and heavily soiled items only
- Turn clothes inside out to preserve color and reduce pilling
- Hang dry when possible (saves energy, extends garment life)
- Don't overload washer/dryer

Stain Removal:
- Treat stains immediately
- Blot, don't rub
- Cold water for blood and protein stains
- Hot water for oil-based stains
- White vinegar for deodorant stains
- Baking soda paste for general stains

Storage:
- Fold knits (hanging stretches them)
- Hang wovens (shirts, pants, dresses)
- Use padded hangers for delicates
- Cedar blocks prevent moths
- Store out-of-season items clean (stains set over time)

=== SUSTAINABLE FABRIC CHOICES ===

Eco-Friendly Options:
- Organic cotton (no pesticides)
- Linen (requires less water to grow)
- Tencel/Lyocell (made from wood pulp, biodegradable)
- Recycled polyester (from plastic bottles)
- Hemp (durable, requires little water)

Avoid When Possible:
- Conventional cotton (heavy pesticide use)
- Virgin polyester (petroleum-based)
- Rayon/Viscose (harsh chemical processing unless certified)

Buy Quality:
- One well-made wool coat > three cheap synthetic ones
- Investment pieces last longer, reduce waste
- Repair and maintain rather than replace
